# PCE Blocks — Simple English Words + Wisdom

> **How to use this file:**
> A "block" is just a small group of words you memorize ONCE and reuse in many questions.
> For each block: I give you the **plain English meaning** (daily-life words), a **tiny story/picture** to remember it, and the **wisdom** = how to turn the keyword into a full sentence in the exam.
> **Big rule:** marks come from *points*, not perfect words. If you remember the idea, write it in any simple English — you still get the mark.

---

## 🔑 The golden trick for ALL blocks
1. The letters (FATP, CIA...) are just a *hook* to pull the words out of your memory.
2. Each word is ONE point.
3. In the exam, take the word → add "means..." → add a tiny example. That's a full answer.

Example: `F = Fairness` → write: *"Fairness means the system treats everyone equally and does not discriminate, e.g. it should not favour men over women."* Done — that's one full point.

## BLOCK 1 — FATP (the 4 data-science ethics rules)
Think of a person you trust. They are **FAir, Take responsibility, Tell the truth, and keep your secrets.**

| Letter | English word | Daily-life meaning (simple) | Picture to remember |
|---|---|---|---|
| **F** | **Fairness** | Treat everyone equal. No favourites. No bias. | A teacher who grades all students by the same rule |
| **A** | **Accountability** | Someone must take the blame / be responsible when it goes wrong. | "Who is responsible?" — a manager who owns the mistake |
| **T** | **Transparency** | Be open. Show how it works. No hiding. | A glass box — you can see inside |
| **P** | **Privacy** | Keep people's personal info secret and safe. | A locked diary |

**Wisdom:** If a question says *"ethics of data / AI principles"* → dump all 4 here. Each word + "means..." + 1 example = 4 easy points.

## BLOCK 2 — CIA (the 3 security goals)
Not the spy agency 🙂. It's what good security must protect.

| Letter | English word | Daily-life meaning (simple) | Picture |
|---|---|---|---|
| **C** | **Confidentiality** | Keep it secret — only the right people can see it. | A password on your phone |
| **I** | **Integrity** | The data is correct and not changed by anyone bad. | A sealed letter — you know nobody opened/edited it |
| **A** | **Availability** | You can use it when you need it (not down/blocked). | An ATM that always works |

**How each is protected (write this to look smart):**
- Confidentiality → **encryption** (scramble the data)
- Integrity → **hashing** (a check that proves nothing changed)
- Availability → **backup** (a spare copy)
- The threat that attacks Availability → **DoS** (attacker floods the system so nobody can use it)

**Wisdom:** Security question? Write the 3 words + the matching protection. Instant strong answer.

## BLOCK 3 — DCVC (the 4 parts of "Informed Consent")
"Informed consent" = the person said **YES**, and they *truly understood* what they said yes to.
Imagine signing a form at the hospital — for it to be fair, 4 things must be true:

| Letter | English word | Daily-life meaning (simple) | Picture |
|---|---|---|---|
| **D** | **Disclosure** | You were TOLD everything first. Nothing hidden. | Doctor explains the risks before surgery |
| **C** | **Comprehension** | You actually UNDERSTOOD it (not confusing words). | Explained in your own language |
| **V** | **Voluntariness** | You chose freely — nobody forced you. | No gun to your head, no pressure |
| **C** | **Competence** | You are ABLE to decide (adult, clear mind). | A child or drunk person can't give real consent |

**Wisdom:** Question about "consent" or "cookies / agreement"? → list all 4. Easy story: "told → understood → free → able."

## BLOCK 4 — AER (3 rights under GDPR)
GDPR = Europe's data-protection law (2018). It gives normal people 3 powers over their own data:

| Letter | English word | Daily-life meaning (simple) | Picture |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | **Access** | "Let me SEE what data you have about me." | Asking a shop for your file |
| **E** | **Erasure** | "DELETE my data." (a.k.a. *right to be forgotten*) | Pressing the trash button |
| **R** | **Rectification** | "FIX the wrong info about me." | Correcting your misspelled name |

**Wisdom:** GDPR question → name the law (EU, 2018) + these 3 rights. Memory hook: **See it, Delete it, Fix it.**

## BLOCK 5 — MPD (3 security threats)
The 3 main bad things that attack a system. Memory hook: **"Bad software, Fake message, Flood."**

| Letter | English word | Daily-life meaning (simple) | Picture |
|---|---|---|---|
| **M** | **Malware** | Bad software (Virus / Worm / Trojan). | A program that infects and harms your PC |
| **P** | **Phishing / spoofing** | A fake message pretending to be someone you trust, to steal info. | Fake "your bank" email asking for your password |
| **D** | **DoS / DDoS** | Flooding a system with traffic so it stops working. | 1000 fake customers blocking a real shop door |

**Wisdom:** "Threats" question → these 3, each with its 1-line example. (Note: this DoS is the same one that attacks **Availability** in CIA — reuse it!)

## BLOCK 6 — MEA (3 good privacy practices in software)
How to build software that respects privacy. Memory hook: **"Collect less, Lock it, Limit who enters."**

| Letter | English word | Daily-life meaning (simple) | Picture |
|---|---|---|---|
| **M** | **Minimisation** | Collect only the little data you really need. | Don't ask birthday if you don't need it |
| **E** | **Encryption** | Scramble the data so thieves can't read it. | Secret code |
| **A** | **Access control** | Only allowed people can open the data. | Key-card door |

**Wisdom:** "Privacy in software / privacy by design" question → these 3. (Encryption appears here AND in CIA — same word, two questions!)

## BLOCK 7 — Laudon principles (the 5 ways to decide right/wrong)
These are 5 thinking tools from the textbook (Laudon). When you face a hard ethics choice, pick one and apply it. You only need to *recognise and briefly explain* them.

| Principle | Daily-life meaning (simple) |
|---|---|
| **Golden Rule** | Treat others how YOU want to be treated. ("Put yourself in their shoes.") |
| **Categorical Imperative** | If it's wrong for everyone to do it, it's wrong for you too. ("What if everybody did this?") |
| **Slippery Slope** | If a small bad action leads to bigger and bigger harm, don't start. ("One step leads to a fall.") |
| **Utilitarian** | Choose the option that does the most good for the most people. |
| **Risk-Aversion** | Avoid the action with the worst possible outcome / highest risk. |
| *(FIP = Fair Information Practices)* | A set of rules for handling personal data fairly. |

**Wisdom:** "How would you decide / which principle applies?" → name 1–2 of these and say WHY. Golden Rule + Utilitarian are the easiest to explain fast.

## BLOCK 8 — Bias examples (your 2 ready-made stories)
Keep these 2 real examples in your pocket. They prove "algorithmic bias" instantly.

| Example | What happened (simple) | Use it for |
|---|---|---|
| **Amazon** | Their AI hiring tool **discriminated against women** (it learned from old male-dominated data). | "AI bias", "fairness" |
| **COMPAS** | A US court risk tool **discriminated against Black people**. | "AI bias", "fairness" |

**Wisdom:** Any question about bias/fairness in AI → drop Amazon + COMPAS. One sentence each = strong, specific answer that examiners love.

## 🧠 Final wisdom — how to "extract the right answer"
1. **Read the question, find ONE trigger word:** ethics→FATP, security→CIA/MPD, consent→DCVC, GDPR→AER, privacy software→MEA, bias→Amazon/COMPAS.
2. **Pull the matching block** from memory using its letters.
3. **Expand each letter** into: *word + "means..." + 1 example.*
4. **Write in bullet points**, not a long essay — points = marks.
5. Don't worry about perfect English. The *idea* gets the mark. Simple words are fine.

**Remember:** there are really only ~6–7 things to learn (the blocks), not 12 questions. Learn the blocks, and the answers build themselves.
