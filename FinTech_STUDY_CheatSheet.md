# FinTech — Memory Cheat Sheet (How to actually pass this)

## The big idea / គំនិតធំ
**Don't memorize 25 full answers.** Memorize the **building blocks** below, then memorize a **short keyword trigger** for each question. In the exam, expand the keywords into sentences in your own words. You get marks for the *points*, not the exact words.

**កុំទន្ទេញចម្លើយ ២៥ ទាំងស្រុង។** ទន្ទេញ **ប្លុក (building blocks)** ខាងក្រោម រួចទន្ទេញ **ពាក្យគន្លឹះខ្លី** សម្រាប់សំណួរនីមួយៗ។ ពេលប្រឡង ពន្លាតពាក្យគន្លឹះទៅជាប្រយោគដោយពាក្យផ្ទាល់ខ្លួន។ គ្រូឱ្យពិន្ទុលើ *ចំណុច* មិនមែនពាក្យពិតប្រាកដ។

## STEP 1 — Learn these 7 building blocks (90% of all answers come from here)

### Block 1 — Bashir's 5 themes (reuse in almost every question)
- **Decentralisation / disintermediation** — peers exchange value "without a centrally trusted arbitrator" (no middleman).
- **Faster dealings + cost-saving** — a shared ledger removes "verification, reconciliation, and clearance."
- **Transparency & trust / immutability** — tamper-proof records.
- **CeFi–DeFi convergence** — banks and crypto/FinTech will *coexist*, not destroy each other.
- **Regulation is the hardest problem** — needs AML / KYC / CFT.

### Block 2 — Cambodia examples (sprinkle these everywhere for marks)
- **Wallets:** Wing, TrueMoney, ABA Mobile, ACLEDA Mobile, Pi Pay
- **Bakong** = National Bank of Cambodia, blockchain-based, interoperable backbone
- **KHQR** = single national QR standard, works across all banks/wallets

### Block 3 — Benefits (memorize the word "CASCI")
**C**onvenience · **A**ccessibility/inclusion · **S**peed · **C**ost-saving · **I**nformation/record-keeping (+ economic growth)

### Block 4 — Risks (memorize "C-F-S-P-E-O")
**C**ybersecurity · **F**raud/scams · **S**ystem failure (single point of failure) · **P**rivacy loss · **E**xclusion (no phone/skills) · **O**ver-reliance + **irreversibility**

### Block 5 — Security (memorize "CIN by E+A")
Goals: **C**onfidentiality · **I**ntegrity · **N**on-repudiation
Tools: **E**ncryption (scrambles data) + **A**uthentication (PIN, biometrics, 2FA)

### Block 6 — Emerging-market challenges (memorize "RTICCL")
**R**egulation · **T**rust · **I**nfrastructure · **C**ybersecurity · **C**apital/scale · **L**iteracy (+ interoperability)

### Block 7 — Blockchain properties (memorize "PDCAU")
**P**eer-to-peer · **D**istributed ledger · **C**ryptographically secure · **A**ppend-only/immutable · **U**pdatable only by consensus
(Blocks chained by hashes → change one block breaks all after it)

## STEP 3 — How to study (the method that works)

1. **Day 1:** Memorize only the 7 Blocks above. Say each acronym out loud until automatic.
2. **Day 2:** Cover the answers. Read each question, say the keyword trigger, then *speak* the full answer aloud. Check. Repeat the ones you miss.
3. **Active recall, not re-reading.** Reading the PDF again feels productive but doesn't stick. Testing yourself does.
4. **Write 3 answers from memory** on paper each session. Writing > reading for exams.
5. **Teach it.** Explain Q6 (banks) or Q21 (stablecoins) to a friend or out loud to yourself. If you can teach it, you know it.
6. **Spaced repetition:** review the questions you got wrong again the next day, not all 25 every time.
7. **Spot the repeats:** notice Q12 = Q5(security part), Q13 = Q23, Q3 = Q14 (Cambodia). Learning one teaches the other.

## Tip / គន្លឹះ
Questions 12 & 23 (security/risks), 13 & 23 (risks), 3 & 14 (Cambodia examples), and 22 (benefits) all reuse the SAME blocks. So you really only have about **10 distinct things to learn**, not 25. / សំណួរទាំងនេះប្រើប្លុកដូចគ្នា ដូច្នេះអ្នកមានរឿងត្រូវរៀនពិតប្រាកដប្រហែល **១០** មិនមែន ២៥ ទេ។
